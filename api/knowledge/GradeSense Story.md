# 🎯 **WHAT GRADESENSE IS**

## **The Surface Level (What It Looks Like Today)**

GradeSense is an AI-powered assessment platform that:

- Automatically evaluates subjective answers (essays, short answers, descriptive questions)
- Generates rubric-aligned feedback
- Reduces teacher grading time by 80%+
- Creates structured performance data at competency level
- Gives students a dashboard to see their results

**Current MVP includes:**

- Grading engine (AI-powered, rubric-based)
- Basic analytics
- Student dashboard (students can see their results)
- Built with a contract developer
- First pilot: Warm lead with a PU college

**At face value it looks like:** "AI that grades papers."

**But that's not the real mission.**

---

## **The Deeper Layer (What It's Actually Solving)**

Traditional education has one structural bottleneck above all others:

**Evaluation bandwidth.**

A teacher with 60-80 students:

- Spends 10-15 hours per week just grading
- Takes 2-3 weeks to return feedback
- Writes "Good effort. Needs improvement." because there's no time for more
- Can't personalize because personalization requires measurement
- Can't measure continuously because measurement is expensive

The system optimizes for:

- ✅ Completing the syllabus
- ✅ Batch uniformity
- ✅ Standardized test scores

It does NOT optimize for:

- ❌ Mastery
- ❌ Continuous improvement
- ❌ Individual learning curves
- ❌ Helping students discover who they are

**GradeSense exists to solve the economic constraint that prevents deep, continuous, individualized evaluation at scale.**

# 💡 **THE SOLUTION**

## **How GradeSense Works (Today)**

### **Step 1: Upload**

Teacher uploads answer sheets (PDF or image).

### **Step 2: Define Rubric**

Teacher inputs 4-6 criteria with point values.  
(Or: GradeSense suggests a rubric based on subject and grade level)

### **Step 3: AI Grades**

GPT-4 evaluates each answer against the rubric.  
Outputs: Score per criterion + 2-3 lines of specific feedback.

### **Step 4: Teacher Reviews**

Teacher sees AI scores with full breakdown.  
Can accept, edit, or reject any score.  
Teacher always has final say.

### **Step 5: Students Get Results**

Dashboard shows:

- Overall score
- Breakdown by criterion
- Specific feedback on what to improve
- Progress over time (as more data accumulates)

### **Time comparison:**

text

```
BEFORE: 50 essays → 10 hours → 2 weeks → "Good effort"
AFTER:  50 essays → 10 minutes → Next day → Detailed breakdown
```

# 🛡️ **DEFENSIBILITY**

## **The 7 Moats:**

### **1. Curriculum-Aligned Evaluation Engine**

Not generic AI grading. CBSE-calibrated, IELTS-calibrated, NEP-aligned.  
The rubric library is a proprietary dataset that takes years to build.  
OpenAI doesn't have 50,000 CBSE essay samples with teacher scores.  
You will.

### **2. Data Flywheel**

More schools → more essays graded → better training data → higher accuracy → more schools.  
At 2M essays: You can tell a student "You're in the 73rd percentile."  
At 10M essays: You can predict board exam scores from Grade 9 mastery data.  
Google starts at zero. You compound.

### **3. Institutional Compliance**

DPDP Act 2023 compliance. Data on Indian servers. Audit trails. Human-in-loop.  
International players (Google, OpenAI) aren't India-first.  
You are. That's a regulatory moat.

### **4. School Integrations**

Embedded in Teachmint, Google Classroom, Classplus workflows.  
Switching means losing 5 years of student data. Nobody does that.

### **5. Longitudinal Learning Analytics**

Track a student from Class 6 to Class 12 (7 years).  
No one else has this data. It literally cannot be bought or replicated quickly.

### **6. Rubric Marketplace + Teacher Community**

Teachers contribute rubrics. Earn passive income. Build reputation.  
10,000 teachers with 3 years invested = community lock-in.

### **7. CBSE/NEP Partnerships**

Work WITH regulators. Help shape evaluation standards.  
If CBSE endorses GradeSense, it's game over for competitors.
