

## Opportunity Mapping: Why APAC, Why Now?

We conducted a comparative analysis of 5 APAC countries using 10+ variables across market size, growth rate, digital behavior, import complexity, and cultural fit. The **Category Development Index (CDI)** further helped identify countries with high per-capita demand but under-penetration by global brands.

|**Country**|**Luggage Market Size**|**Growth Rate**|**CDI**|**Verdict**|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|**Singapore**|~$650M|~13%|2.47|High-margin early launchpad|
|**South Korea**|~$1.8B|~15%|0.78|Large-scale growth market|
|**Hong Kong**|~$675M|~12%|2.02|Premium + tourism-friendly|
|**Australia**|~$1.2B|~7.6%|1.00|Affluent but expensive|
|**Japan**|~$5.3B|~3%|0.96|Highly competitive, mature|

**Why Singapore & South Korea?**

Singapore offers frictionless entry, high disposable income, and a large Indian diaspora — a perfect “trust bridge” market. South Korea brings scale, digital-first infrastructure, and trend-responsiveness, but demands **localization** in brand tone, language, and speed.

To chart Mokobara’s APAC expansion, we studied three global D2C brands, **Away**, **July**, and **Herschel,** each successful in entering premium luggage or travel gear markets through differentiated positioning and localized execution. Their playbooks offer actionable insights for Mokobara’s international journey.

### **Away: Selling Stories, Not Just Suitcases**

Away built its success by transforming luggage into a lifestyle product — minimal design, strong storytelling, and emotional resonance. It targets urban professionals and millennials who see travel as part of their identity.

- **Key Features**: Built-in USB, scratch-resistant shell, lifetime warranty
- **GTM**: D2C-first, with pop-ups in cities like Singapore and influencer-led campaigns
- **APAC Wins**: 15% SG premium market share, 400% YoY TikTok growth in Southeast Asia
- **Lesson for Mokobara**: Build an emotional, value-driven narrative and test early with pop-ups and creator-led storytelling.

### **July: Minimalism Meets Monogramming**

July focused on product precision and design purity, just a few SKUs, done exceptionally well. It positioned itself as the aesthetic-first brand with personalization options and a calm, premium vibe.

- **Key Features**: Aerospace-grade shell, color drops, monogramming
- **GTM**: Localized D2C website, 1–2 day delivery via Coupang (KR) and regional pop-ups
- **APAC Wins**: #1 luggage search brand in Australia; 3x D2C growth in SG
- **Lesson for Mokobara**: Prioritize SKU discipline, fast fulfillment, and design-focused digital campaigns.

### **Herschel: Global Lifestyle, Local Touches**

Herschel scaled through a blend of mass appeal and premium design. Rather than relying on storytelling or minimalism, it expanded via collaborations and selective retail placements.

- **Key Features**: Signature striped liners, laptop-friendly layouts, seasonal editions
- **GTM**: Mix of D2C, flagship stores, and premium 3P platforms
- **APAC Moves**: Collabs with Apple Japan, selective retail in HK and SG
- **Lesson for Mokobara**: Use cultural cues and curated partnerships (e.g., Planet Traveller SG, Coupang SK) to build presence without going mass.

**Key Lessons for Mokobara** All three brands succeeded not just because of great products, but because they **adapted their messaging, delivery speed, and pricing strategy to each region**. Mokobara’s edge lies in its aesthetic-meets-function design, Indian-origin authenticity, and D2C agility, all of which must now be tuned for APAC realities like fast shipping, influencer validation, and tech-enabled features.

## 3-Year GTM Plan: From Trust to Scale

Our expansion strategy follows a **phased rollout** across high-potential APAC markets, beginning with Singapore and South Korea, chosen for their digital maturity, high premium spend, and Indian brand familiarity (SG) or growth potential (KR). The roadmap balances early traction with long-term scale.

### **Phase 1 (0–12 Months) – Singapore Launch**

_**Revenue Goal: ₹12–15 Cr**_

- Focus: Diaspora-heavy, English-speaking, high-margin market
- Channels: Shopee, Lazada, D2C website, premium pop-ups (e.g., Changi Airport)
- Marketing: Instagram-first storytelling, creator collabs, localized content
- Logistics: 1–2 day fulfillment via Ninja Van or Janio
- Goal: Build brand equity, refine international ops playbook

### **Phase 2 (12–24 Months) – South Korea Entry**

_**Revenue Goal: ₹30–35 Cr**_

- Focus: Scale, digitally native shoppers, influencer culture
- Channels: Coupang (Rocket delivery), Naver Smart Store
- Marketing: Korean-language creator UGC, K-lifestyle positioning
- Ops: KR-language CX, local payment methods, fast returns
- Goal: Drive top-line growth through design + delivery excellence

### **Phase 3 (24–36 Months) – Premium Hubs (HK/AU)**

_**Revenue Goal: ₹45–50 Cr**_

- Focus: Travel-retail hubs with affluent buyers
- Channels: Lane Crawford (HK), Tangs (SG), Airport retail, D2C
- Marketing: High-aspiration messaging, gift sets, limited editions
- Goal: Maximize AOV + visibility via curated presence, not mass scale

## Strategic Levers, Risks, and Why This Will Work

To succeed in APAC, Mokobara must activate the right levers and stay alert to market-specific execution risks. Below is a snapshot of both, and how we recommend responding.

### Strategic Levers

- **India-Origin Advantage**: Mokobara can proudly own the narrative of a brand that blends **Indian craftsmanship with global quality,** a positioning still underrepresented in APAC.
- **Design-Led Differentiation**: Functional elegance, smooth wheels, TSA locks, and tech touchpoints make the product both stylish and practical, ideal for aspirational Gen Z/Millennials.
- **D2C Expertise**: Proven digital brand-building in India gives Mokobara a head start in navigating APAC’s e-commerce platforms (Shopee, Coupang, TikTok Shop).
- **Influencer Storytelling**: Targeting micro-influencers (5K–50K) in each region builds early traction with low CAC and trust-led conversions.

---

### Key Risks & Recommendations

|**Risk**|**Response**|
|---|---|
|1–2 day delivery expectations|Partner with regional players like Rocket Delivery (KR), Ninja Van (SG)|
|New brand = trust gap|Lean into reviews, creator collabs, UGC-rich social proof|
|Language & cultural barriers|Localized content + native language CX + minimal returns friction|
|SKU mismatch|Start with small, proven set → test local preferences via pop-ups|
|High CAC|Prioritize organic influencer seeding before paid performance blitz|
